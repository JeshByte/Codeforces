The main focus is on the digits of the numbers. If the final answer has atleast m+1 digits, then Sasha will win or else Anna wins. The optimal strategy for Anna will be to choose the number with most number of trailing zeroes, so that when it gets reversed Sasha loses out on those digits. On the other hand, the optimal strategy for Sasha will be to take the number with most number of trailing zeroes and the number with most number of digits before its trailing zeroes(if any) and concatenate them to protect the zeroes of the former number from Anna. So here the numbers itself dont matter, we just need the number of trailing zeroes and the number digits before them. So we can simulate this game by using two multisets where we will push the above mentioned values for each element. One set will keep track of numbers with most trailing zeroes and the other one will keep track of numbers with most digits before its trailing zeroes. As the total moves will be at most 2n, it will be well within the constraints.
