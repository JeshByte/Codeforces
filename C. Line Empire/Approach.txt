So there are two ways to conquer kingdoms. One is to stay at one place and conquer the later kingdoms with b cost(b-type move). And another one is to move to the conquered kingdom after conquering it with cost a and then repeating the cycle(a-type move). The optimal answer would be a mix of these. My hyposthesis is that once you start doing the b-type move, it is of no use to do the a-type move. Because lets say you are at kingdom a1. You perform b-type move on a2 and then a-type move on a3. It doesn't make sense becuase if you had to incur the additional cost of a, you should have moved to the closer kingdom a2 first and then tried to conquer a3. So basically you will perform a series of a-type moves till some kingdom and then the rest of them will be conquered through b-type moves. We can go through each kingdom and check what would be the cost if it is used as the breakpoint, and then the least one is our answer. We will use prefix and suffix sums for fast computation of the cost.
