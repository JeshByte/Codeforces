So if we look at the numbers bitwise, we will observe that for a position if all the bits are set then it is ok and their order doesn't matter. But if all the bits are not set than the first and last number must be having unset bits at that position and the rest doesn't matter. So only those numbers whose bits are unset in the above mentioned positions can be placed in the first and last position. The middle portion can have the numbers in any position as it won't matter. Lets say there are r such numbers that could be placed in first/last position. So the final formula is (ways of taking and arranging 2 numbers from r numbers)*(number of ways of arranging remaining n-2 numbers) which equals to r*(r-1)*(n-2)!. This will give us our final answer.
