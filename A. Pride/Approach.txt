When you find gcd of 2 adjacent elements, there are 2 cases. First case is you get 1. Then you just need convert the other numbers using this one. The other case is you get a non-one gcd. Then further calculating the gcd of these 2 elements is of no use as it will give you the same gcd again and again. You should then take this new element and try to find its gcd with the next element and so on till you get one. Lets say you find a subarray whose gcd is 1. Then the answer will be (length of subarray-1)+n-1. Obviously to optimise this we need to find the shortest subarray with gcd 1 which we can easily do as the constraints allow a n^2 solution. But this assumes that you will always need to perform the operation at least once to get a 1. Is that always true? No, there can already be a 1 in the array. In that case the answer will be n-(count of 1s).
