So because the sequence needs to be lexicographically smallest, at every step we should always try to go for the smallest node. Thus we need to follow a greedy strategy here. After reaching a node, we should try to go for the smallest reachable node from there. But we can't consider only the direct neighbours of the current node. The notebook doesn't keep track of duplicate nodes. So we have the option of backtracking to already visited nodes if it allows us to go to some other node of smaller value. So what we can do is to traverse through the graph and whenever we reach a node, we push all of it's neighbours into a min heap. Then we will traverse the smallest element from that heap. The heap here keeps track of all the unvisited nodes that can be traversed - directly or indirectly. The order of traversal of nodes following this logic will give us our answer. Also the edges given to us consist of self-loops and duplicate edges. But they are of no use, so just remove them before further processing.
