So for n to be a divisor of k^n, it needs to have all the prime factors of n raised to >= powers in n. As k^n, is just k multiplied n times, k itself needs to have all the prime factors of n. Now lets say a prime factor p is raised to power t in n. Lets say the same p is raised to t2 in k. Then for this p, t2*n>=t which give us t2=Ceil(t,n). So then we multiply p^t2 into our current k. We will have to do this for each p of n. We can do these efficiently in n*logn time.
