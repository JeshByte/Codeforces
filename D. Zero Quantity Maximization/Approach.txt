Every element has a unique d to make it zero. We can simply keep track of the d with max frequency to get our answer. But the d can be in decimal which will cause precision issues. So we will store it in the form of reduced fractions(which could be done by dividing the values by their gcd). Also when ai=0, d is undefined so we will ignore those elements. When both ai=bi=0 then these will always be 0, and we need to keep track of them seperately and add it to the maximum frequency that we find. We also need to consider the case of not applying the operation(which is basically choosing d=0), in which case the answer will be count of zeroes in b. The maximum of both of these values will give us our final answer.
